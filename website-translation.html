What is Website Localization Process?

Most of the times, producing the translation of a web-copy can have a dispersion issue on its overall layout and formatting. Indeed most apparently, Sometimes, there are RTL (Right-toLeft) languages. In order to get those languages localized in terms of re-arranging the webimages, script buttons, and all other factors, additional hand-on skills and approach is required.

Also, in both RTL (Right-to-Left) and LTR (Left-to-Right) languages, translated text may be overspreading and squeezing compared to the source text of the website which ultimately affects the overall design and flow of the website. Since, we have seen that some languages have longer grammatical and sentence structure than the other. For example, German, Spanish, French and Italian are considered to be wordier than others. Similarly, words and phrases in German ultimately produce 40% over-spreading than its English original content. On the other hands, Mandarin translation produces less no. of words comparing to English equivalent. These difference of sizes of the languages text from one language to the other language can be challenging to maintaining the same design and theme of the website. Which necessarily needs to implement major adjustments and revision to ensure proper stabilization of the website in overall design process.
